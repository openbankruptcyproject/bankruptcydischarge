Bankruptcy Discharge Timeline

Chapter 7 discharge: approximately 3-4 months. Chapter 13 discharge: after completing 3-5 years of plan payments.

Chapter 7 Timeline

EventTiming
Petition filedDay 0
341 Meeting of Creditors30 -- 45 days after filing
Deadline for discharge objections60 days after 341 meeting
Debtor education certificate dueBefore discharge (typically filed by day 60)
Discharge order enteredApproximately 60 -- 90 days after 341 meeting
Case closedShortly after discharge (no-asset cases)

Total time from filing to discharge: approximately 3 to 4 months for a straightforward consumer Chapter 7.

Chapter 13 Timeline

EventTiming
Petition filedDay 0
341 Meeting of Creditors30 -- 45 days after filing
Plan payments begin30 days after filing
Plan confirmation hearing2 -- 6 months after filing
Plan payments completed36 -- 60 months after filing
Discharge order enteredAfter all payments, certifications filed

Total time from filing to discharge: 3 to 5 years, depending on your income relative to the state median.

What Can Delay the Discharge

  • Missing debtor education certificate -- The court will not enter discharge without it
  • Objections to discharge -- Creditors, the trustee, or UST may object under Section 727(a)
  • Adversary proceedings -- Lawsuits challenging dischargeability of specific debts
  • Failure to appear at 341 meeting -- Case may be dismissed
  • Undisclosed assets -- Trustee investigation may delay case closing
  • Means test issues -- UST motion to dismiss for abuse in Chapter 7

To avoid delays: Complete your debtor education course early, attend the 341 meeting, disclose all assets truthfully, and respond promptly to any requests from the trustee.
